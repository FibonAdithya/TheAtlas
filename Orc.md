- Orcs descend from the duel between Eofor and Ozymandias, high in the Kingarrock Spine. From violence and death they originated, and so there they will return. In youth, orcs begin to have clear visions of the moment of their death. This is always intentional and often violent - no orc dies of natural causes.
- When you choose to play an orc character, you'll tell the DM what your moment of death looks like, and they'll write it into the story. At some point in your journey, you may begin to recognise similarities to your visions and realise that you're arriving at that moment. In this final blaze of glory, all your rolls are automatically nat 20s. However, once the moment comes, the character is irrevocably destroyed.
- Orcs look like hammerhead goblins from the Edge Chronicles! best art to ever art. They're not as bulky as normal fantasy orcs - more lithe and gangly, but they are predisposed to put on muscle faster than humans for the same amount of training. So you will see the average orc fighter be more muscly than your average human fighter.
- Also, not necessarily green-skinned! In fact more commonly the colour of an angry bruise or swollen vein, i.e. dark purple / red. When they get angry or blush, they blush redder than humans.
