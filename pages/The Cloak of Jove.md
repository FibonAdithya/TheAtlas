  "When the most curious of these first animals poked above the surface of the sea, they were dismayed to find none of their myths were true. All the they had imagined was false, and the empty waves stretched in each direction until storms or fog blocked the horizon. 'Then we will leave these tales here, because we have no need for lies,' and with that each turned in a separate direction, and told their favourite story. 
  
  The stories were not true, of course. But as the words travelled out across the ocean with nobody to check them, and long after the storytellers dove back down below, it mattered less and less whether they were real or not. Here on this coast, we must be so far away that it doesn't matter at all whether we are real, and we don't benefit from worrying much about it. 
  
  Our name for the teller of the Candle Coast is Jove. This cloak is a piece of its tongue, and contains its love of stories."
