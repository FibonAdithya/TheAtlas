- Timeline:
	- The world has multiple [[Ages]]
	- The current era is the [[New Age]] following from the [[Great War]]
- Creation myth:
	- Aldran was created by the 3 primal dragons: [[Salark]], [[Nalark]] and [[Talark]]
	- [[Salark]] betrays his siblings, killing [[Nalark]] whos blood becomes the [[Rings of Nalark]]
	- [[Talark]] imprisons [[Salark]] becoming the mortal plane while [[Salark]] become the [[World Below]]
- Truths of the World:
	- <b> The War is Over (kind of) </b>
		- #+BEGIN_QUOTE
		  "Peace is what we call war while it's catching it's breath"
		  #+END_QUOTE
		- Everyone knows the [[Treaty of the Crown]] ended open warfare, not hostility. [[Nations]] exist because the empire collapsed, not because stability was achieved. Armies shrink, but do not disappear. Borders are inked in anticipation of erasure. The [[Unsea]], once Zilangro, sits at the continent’s heart as proof that wars no longer need armies to end nations.
	- <b> The Wyrdmarked Guilds monopolise industry</b>
		- #+BEGIN_QUOTE
		  "Blood builds the world"
		  #+END_QUOTE
		- The eight guild houses of Metrol dominate industry because their bloodlines make them uniquely capable of doing so. Wyrdmarks appear when they will, grant what they will, and bind families to function. Monopoly is tolerated not because it is loved, but because no alternative could exist.
	- <b> The Sovereign Endless </b>
		- #+BEGIN_QUOTE
		  "They do not rule us  - they are the rules.”
		  #+END_QUOTE
		- The [[Sovereign Endless]] are not gods in the mortal sense, nor do they require belief. They are the Sovereign principles of existence made manifest. Worship is transactional, cultural, and personal, not centralised. No one doubts their reality; debate exists only in interpretation. When planes converge and domains assert themselves, people do not ask if the Endless are involved - only how.
	- <b> Magic is shallow and wide</b>
		- #+BEGIN_QUOTE
		  "Many can light lamps. Few can move the sun"
		  #+END_QUOTE
		- Magic permeates daily life through Elusium: transport, industry, communication, comfort. This is normal. What is not normal is magic that rewrites fate, restores the dead, or reshapes nations. Such acts demand rare materials, divine petitions, or unbearable cost.
	- <b> Evils both ancient and modern act upon the world </b>
		- #+BEGIN_QUOTE
		  "The past does not die. It only waits.
		  #+END_QUOTE
		- Threats do not belong to a single age. Demonlords, god-kings, mercenary companies, political cabals, and private armies all exert pressure simultaneously. No one believes evil was defeated with the empire. Power shifts forms, recruits new hands, and waits for opportunity.
	- <b> There are no more great men </b>
		- #+BEGIN_QUOTE
		  The Emperor was the last great man. Now only machinery remains.
		  #+END_QUOTE
		- History no longer turns on singular legends. The fall of the Aratin Emperor marked the end of an age where individuals could reshape the world by will alone. In the present era, outcomes are determined by treaties, guilds, doctrines, supply chains, planar cycles, and institutions too vast for any one person to master. Heroes may act, tyrants may rise, but neither defines the future - they are absorbed, redirected, or erased by the systems they move within.
