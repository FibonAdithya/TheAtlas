- Dark age, grim fantasy analogue.
- [[Firepox]] plague -
	- Firepox is a near-certain death sentence. The name comes from the small blooming sores it causes, which cover the entire body. The skin of a firepox sufferer swells and cracks and features many pustules and blisters. The disease also includes a high fever. Spellcasters combust upon death
- [[Elderglass]].
	- It resembles glass, though it varies wildly in appearance, with everything from dark shades of scarlet to luminescent shades that glimmer even after dark. Elderglass is, to the best of knowledge, indestructible, yet has proven versatile in its use. Braided fibers of Elderglass are flexible enough to form rope bridges that span chasms that engineers would be hard pressed to cover. Massive towers of Elderglass also stretch into the sky, impervious to even the harshest 
	  	  winds and storms. Some Elderglass, like that found in absorbs light by day and glows in the evening when the sun sets, during the time known as Elderlight. The methods of creating Elderglass - or even manipulating - is currently beyond the knowledge of all mortal races.
	- Possibly predates the empire.
- [[Aratin Empire]]
	- Vor'ak is the land of the Emperor, [[Metrol]] was once the northern half. The people of Vor'ak look back fondly on the Empire, and view the current age as the fall of order. While surrounded by enemies from both the south and west, and no military might to speak off Vor'ak sustains only through the defenses left by the elders.
	- The [[King's Dark Lanterns]] are an order of knights who travel from village to village performing purification and witch burnings.
- Truths:
	- **The elders keep the nation standing**
		- #+BEGIN_QUOTE
		  "We are the moss that grows on the bones of giants".
		  #+END_QUOTE
		- The people of Vor’ak do not believe in their own agency. They live in a world they didn't build and cannot fix. Every defensive wall is a ruin they've patched, and every weapon is a relic they've polished. While other nations innovate, Vor’ak clings to the [[Elderglass]] with a religious, protective fervor, knowing that if the ancient Spires ever fail, they would fall too.
	- **Magic is dangerous**
		- #+BEGIN_QUOTE
		  "To cast is to kindle. Magic only burns.
		  #+END_QUOTE
		- In Vor’ak, magic is a biological threat. Due to the Firepox, spellcasting is viewed as the first symptom of a lethal contagion. While a mage from Tor Leyemil sees a spell as a tool/science, a Vor'aki sees it as a curse. They might physically recoil from a healer, fearing they will also contract the pox.
	- **The King of Vor'ak**
		- #+BEGIN_QUOTE
		  "Heavy is the head that holds the crown."
		  #+END_QUOTE
		- The Emperor was from Vor'ak and his spirit still remains. Those of power and authority who cross the border feel the call of the Crown of Stars, a crown that makes them infused with the lives and memories of all kings before them.
	- **The King's Dark Lanterns**
		- #+BEGIN_QUOTE
		  "Before he was the Emperor, he was our king."
		  #+END_QUOTE
		- In a land where a single infected mage can ignite a village or a monster of the night can turn a family, there is no room for trial or nuance. Suspicion is treated as guilt because the alternative is extinction. Law in Vor’ak is about "burning out the rot" before it spreads. The [[King's Dark Lanterns]] are an order of knights who travel from village to village performing purification and witch burnings.
