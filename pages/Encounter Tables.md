- we want random encounters to be a tool for picking up the tension when things are slowing down, or when the players make a mistake. As a rule of thumb, i want to only be using this about once a session, and increasing this if raphael creates any more explosions.
-
- as a general design philosophy i want the roll to make them think " oh shit we've got to make a decision to run or fight, and the fight will probably be hard ". ideally, we balance it so that they spend time weighing up their decision. Winnable fight, if they still have adequate resources.
- "2 is always a dragon, and 12 is always a wizard"
-
- "8-11 are from the area you're in, 3-6 are generically from the dungeon"
- "7 is the dragon's elite, come to hunt down the party. They are in place 7 because this slot represents something actively hunting the group"
-
- "And because the dragon represents the biggest global fear symbol for our players, it's easy to fill in 3-6. 3 is a runner-up dragon, or the dragon's lieutenant. 4 is a horde/ army of the dragon's minions. 5 is a horrific construct/abomination that the dragon has called forth or cobbled together. 6 is a dragon-hunter, come to kill the beast, and the enemy of my enemy is not necessarily my friend. essentially a dangerous but not (necessarily) outright hostile NPC.
-
- Then the remaining slots are just the same, but for the area we are in: 8 - 11. To keep the probs in check, we mirror it on 7. 11 is our area specific lieutentant/champion, or the area's specific 'dragon'. 10 is a horde, 9 is a monstrosity/experiment, but 8 is another party hunter from backstory, a ghost from one of their pasts.
-
- 8 and 6 bring NPCs or at least backstory relevant people into the mix. We already have bonjocovic, graine, ferris mueler and now Josip is possible too. feel very free to make 8 be a mutinous goblin or any faction member looking to take down the dragon. similarly, if there's a good candidate, it's probably even better to make 8 be a previously seen NPC. but we always fill in the table because the point of it is so that if we don't want to think about it, we dont have to
