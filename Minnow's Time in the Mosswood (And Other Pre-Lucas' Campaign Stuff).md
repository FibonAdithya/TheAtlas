- Minnow's Time in the Mosswood:
- The powerful forest spirit who leads the elves of the Mosswood is called Annuëtheon (Ann-oo-eth-ay-on). They take the form of a giant armoured stag, with many magic items hanging from their antlers like ornaments. When the whole group encountered them, they told you all they knew about Vord, and gave you each a magical gift to help on your journey.
  Annuëtheon wished to speak specifically to Minnow about a sickness spreading through the forest. A corruption in a section to the northwest of the forest has begun to spread, and crucially they can no longer commune with the wildlife in this area, rendering them 'blind' to what is going on inside. More to the point - several patrols of elves have been killed near to the border of the rot, and Annuëtheon's innate ability to sense magic has picked up a strange and old type of sorcery that they haven't seen in a long time. Specifically, the great stag senses a certain kinship between the magic sensed at the murder sites, and around Minnow. Suspicious but giving Minnow the benefit of the doubt, Annuëtheon asks them to stay behind for a day or two to investigate further.
- The upshot of the conversation is as follows:
  The animals in the corrupted section of the forest are no longer under Annuëtheon's control, but they are behaving so unusually that it's assumed they are under the control of something. 
  Though the elves in the forest are well-trained, there are never any other casualties at the scene.
  Much of the magic that the stag has sensed lingers on the wounds left in the elves, and Annuëtheon reports feeling a similar magic on some birds in the rotted forest once or twice.
  All valuables and golden sections of armour are taken from the elves, but none of their clothes or more mundane equipment.
  Gold coins are always left in the eyes of the dead elves, and they've been left lying on their back with their arms crossed like in a sarcophagus.
