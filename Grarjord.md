- Primary goal: Maintain stability and avoid deep entanglement, but protect close allies and safeguard economic interests.
- Early war:
	- Attempted neutrality.
	- Entered war to support [[Korvaish]] against [[M'angas]] border skirmishes.
	- Relied on feudal levies for military strength; industrial development still in infancy.
- Late war:
	- Growing industry demanded mineral and ore imports.
	- Economic ties with [[M'angas]] strengthened despite earlier conflict.
	- Resulted in [[M'angas]] reducing ore exports to [[Tol Leyemil]], disrupting their arcane weapon production.
- Consequences:
	- Strained relations with [[Tol Leyemil]].
	- Maintained strong alliance with [[Korvaish]].
	- Seen as pragmatic but untrustworthy by some nations.
- 3 Truths -
	- <b> Spluttering Industry </b>
		- #+BEGIN_QUOTE
		  "It rattles like a dying dragon and breathes more soot than air, but it’s Grarjordian. I’d rather trust a gear that might fail than a Guild contract that’s designed to.""
		  #+END_QUOTE
		- To avoid over-reliance on [[Metrol]] and the [[Wyrdmarked guilds]] , the King has commissioned state-run industrial hubs.  Because Grarjord lacks the refined bloodline-monopolies of the Guilds, their machinery is often "crude" or experimental. They trade efficiency for independence. This leads to frequent industrial accidents, "Elusium leaks", and a black market for stolen Guild secrets.
	- <b> The Sword becomes the Pen</b>
		- #+BEGIN_QUOTE
		  "Our King wears a crown, but he wields a quill. He doesn't command armies anymore; he manages liabilities. Instead of knights we have accountants in plate armour."
		  #+END_QUOTE
		- The King of Grarjord views himself as a traditional sovereign, but the [[Treaty of the Crown]] has turned kings into administrators. There is a growing movement of "Traditionalists" who want to ignore the treaty and return to individual glory, vs. "Systematists" who know that breaking the treaty would mean total economic collapse.
	- **Ethics of Pragmatism**
		- #+BEGIN_QUOTE
		  "We can be the 'good guys' or we can stay alive"
		  #+END_QUOTE#
		- Grarjord's view on neutrality and pragmatism comes with cost of secrets from allies (perhaps secretly mining timber from Korvaish?). If push comes to shove they will not hesitate to turn on all of their alliies
