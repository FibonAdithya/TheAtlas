- A dead-end in terms of the breaking-kirk's main goal, breeding a rare type of cave beetle for size and strength proved useful in other ways. Rust monsters in the candle coast look a lot like giant rhino beetles.
- They sport a forked prong horn at their head, which vibrates in a mysterious interaction with oxidising metals. In close proximity, they can corrode these metals to powder in seconds, using an anteater-like tongue to mop up the metal as food. The prong seems to have magnetic properties, sensing the metal and even drawing it close to the rust monster.
- Though black in colour most often, rust monsters are usually covered in a thick coating of the rust of whichever metal is most abundant in their area. Hence, they may look orange if around an iron vein, or turquoise-green is copper is their closest food source.
