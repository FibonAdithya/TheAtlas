- There are five Crown Cities at the time of the Second Age (approx Y700 - Y1150). These are spread out along the coast, but as there are three very geologically distinct areas, we can group them a bit. 
  
  The far north-western reaches of the habitable coast are arid, oceanic grassland steppe butting up against the coast in impressive sandy cliffs. The sand sparkles and shifts from gold through purples, greens and blues in mesmerising bands that blur in the heatwaves. The land of big skies, it's called - thin clouds shoot across the views and never bring any rain. An obstinate calm embodies the weather in these parts, and looking north or west from these regions one can see for tens or hundreds of miles which will not appear on any map. Apart from the two cities in this region, settlement is temporary and opportunistic. Yosske and Halicarnassus are the twin hearts of this region.

- So far, we've been in a medieval europe analogue area, sitting in the bones of the Empire and really struggling to keep the flames of civilisation alight. It has the up-and-coming Axemouth to the East, the mighty Kingarrock out West in the mountains, and to the Southwest the mysterious and silent twin city, Glaugarrock. Various capillary towns and farming settlements support these bastions but there are no borders or land disputes; in the Second Age, nobody owns that much land.
