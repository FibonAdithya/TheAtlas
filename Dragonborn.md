- Dragonborn are the glint of an especially powerful dragon's hoard given animus. The shine in a chalice may reflect back the dragon's likeness as it surveys its wealth - and such may be the dragon's power that it does not stop reflecting when the dragon turns to leave. A particularly beautiful reflection might even leave the shining object, and venture out into the world as a dragonborn. Such an object is left matte and lacklustre, which causes the draconic owners to seek out and recollect any wayward dragonborn that may have wandered out of their hoards.
- A dragonborn is likely a full reflection in a pristine mirror, shield or helmet. They retain the correct proportions and even beauty of the creature whose reflection they are. Kobold, on the other hand, may be the reflection in a coin, uncut gem, or shattered mirror. Far more numerous, and often too weak to escape their object without the dragon's help, they are often summoned as an expendable army when the dragons needs one in a pinch. However, since this leaves all the coins and gems without their shine, it's done reluctantly.
